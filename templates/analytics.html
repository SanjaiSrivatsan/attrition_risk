Key Insights & Recommendations

🎯 Top Risk Factors

The analysis reveals that monthly income, overtime, and job satisfaction are the strongest predictors of employee attrition. Focus retention strategies on improving these factors.

💼 Work-Life Balance Impact

Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition rates. Consider implementing better work-life balance policies and monitoring overtime hours more closely.

📈 Tenure Patterns

Employees with less than 2 years at the company are at highest risk. Strengthen onboarding programs and early-career development initiatives.

🎓 Career Development

Years since last promotion strongly correlates with attrition risk. Implement clear career progression paths and regular promotion opportunities.

💰 Compensation Strategy

Monthly income remains a critical factor. Regular salary reviews and competitive compensation packages can significantly reduce attrition.

🔍 Predictive Actions

Immediate actions:
• Conduct stay interviews with high-risk employees
• Review and adjust compensation for underperforming departments
• Implement flexible work arrangements
• Create mentorship programs for new hires
• Regular pulse surveys to monitor job satisfaction
